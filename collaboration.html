Collaboration

The Convection and Urban Meteorology Group has (had) collaborated with other domestic and overseas scientists/groups to advance our understanding of atmospheric convection/waves and urban fluid dynamics.

  • Prof. Hye-Yeong Chun, Yonsei University, Korea

    Convectively forced gravity wave drag parametrization, typhoon-induced gravity waves

  • Prof. Harindra J. S. Fernando, Arizona State University, U.S.A.

    Laboratory and numerical modeling of urban flow and dispersion

  • Prof. Marko Princevac, University of California, Riverside, U.S.A.

    Laboratory and numerical modeling of urban flow and dispersion

  • Prof. Siegfried Raasch, University of Hannover, Germany

    Large-eddy simulation of urban flow and dispersion

  • Prof. Wenbo Tang, Arizona State University, U.S.A.

    Locating an atmospheric contamination source using slow manifolds

  • Prof. Alexander Khain, The Hebrew University of Jerusalem, Israel

    Cloud and precipitation modeling using a cloud model with explicit bin microphysical processes
We welcome collaboration. Some visiting scientist programs, including postdoctoral fellowship program, are available through the Korea Science and Engineering Foundation, the BK21 program or occasionally our group's research funds. Please contact Prof. Jong-Jin Baik.
